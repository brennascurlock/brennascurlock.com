What are Google Documents?

A particularly useful feature of Gmail (Google's email service) is Google docs. Google docs allows you to create, upload, edit, save and share Word documents, Excel spreadsheets and Powerpoint presentations. You will also have the capacity to store any file type on your Google account for easy access.

Course goals

In this Google docs crash course, you can expect to learn the following things:

  • How to access your Google documents and files
  • How to create a document with Google docs
  • How to upload and store your existing documents and files
  • How to share your documents and files with others

To begin this tutorial, you will need the following:

  • Internet access
  • A Gmail (Google email) account
  • A Word, Powerpoint or Excel file to practice uploading

Follow the instructions provided below if you do not have an existing Gmail account. As you learn to access, create, store and share, you should follow along on your Google docs page to get the hang of things. When you're ready, move on to Part 1: Access. Once you have completed all four parts of the lesson, there will be a short assessment to test your skills.

Create a Google account

  1. Go to mail.google.com
  2. Click on "Create an account"
  3. Enter the relevant information
  4. Click "I accept. Create my account."
